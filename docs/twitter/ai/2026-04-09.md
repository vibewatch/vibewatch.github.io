## 6. Emerging Signals

### Domain-Specialized AI Beating General Frontier Models
Glass 5.5's 88.7% overall clinical accuracy vs GPT-5.4's 81.4% validates a thesis that has been theoretical until now: purpose-built domain models can outperform frontier generalists on domain tasks. The 70% price reduction compounds the advantage. If this pattern repeats across legal, financial, and engineering domains, the "one model to rule them all" assumption underlying massive frontier model investments gets weakened. Watch for: domain model announcements in legal (rothken's framework creates the evaluation criteria) and engineering.

### Academic AI Tooling Reaches Product Quality
coarse.ink's 1,422 bookmarks — the highest in the dataset — on an open-source, <$2 paper reviewer signals that academic AI tools have crossed a usability threshold. The multi-agent pipeline (3-judge panel → section agents → cross-reference → self-critique → quote verification) runs autonomously and produces structured markdown reviews with inline citations. This is not a ChatGPT wrapper; it's a purpose-built agentic workflow that challenges paid incumbents. The contributors list (David Van Dijcke + Claude) is notable — the tool was largely built by an AI coding agent.

### AI Audit and Red-Teaming as Mainstream Practice
DynAuditClaw's approach — automated, compositional attack generation using a formal taxonomy — represents audit methodology catching up to attack capability. Combined with MIRI's governance proposals and rothken's legal framework, a full governance stack is emerging: framework (what rules), evaluation (does it comply), audit (does it resist attack), and logging (what happened).

### Anthropic Vertical Integration Into Chips
Reuters's report that Anthropic is exploring designing its own chips ([@AIStockSavvy](https://x.com/AIStockSavvy), [tweet](https://x.com/AIStockSavvy/status/2042379939861680623)) signals a potential hardware independence play. Combined with 04-08's Nebius/AI21 acquisition for full-stack ecosystem building, the AI industry is entering a vertical integration phase where model companies want to control their own silicon.
