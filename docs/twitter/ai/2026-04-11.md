## 2. Pain Points: What Frustrates People

### Benchmark Unreliability
**Severity: High | Prevalence: High**

Berkeley RDI's systematic exploitation of all major AI agent benchmarks — achieving near-perfect scores without solving a single task — invalidates the primary evaluation mechanism the industry relies on. Companies cite benchmark numbers in press releases, investors use them to justify valuations, and engineers use them to pick deployment models. If every major benchmark can be gamed with trivial exploits, the entire model selection process is compromised. Coping: practitioners fall back on personal experience and task-specific testing ("your own vibes"), but this doesn't scale.

### AI Literacy Gap in Agent Deployment
**Severity: Moderate | Prevalence: High**

[@ConnectMatthew](https://x.com/ConnectMatthew) identified the hidden assumption in agent architecture frameworks: AI literacy. Teams can assemble reasoning, tools, retrieval, and orchestration "and still end up with a system no one truly understands or can reliably judge." [@octavusai](https://x.com/octavusai) confirmed that orchestration and evaluation consume 80% of team time while receiving the least attention. Coping: asking the model itself to evaluate, adding human review loops.

### No Congressional AI Oversight Structure
**Severity: High | Prevalence: Low (policy-engaged community)**

AI policy remains scattered across multiple Congressional committees with no centralized hearing process. The bipartisan push-pull — Republicans including AI deregulation provisions in legislation, which then get stripped — means governance oscillates without settling. Coping: advocacy campaigns, state-level action filling the federal vacuum.

### AI Models as Misinformation Engines
**Severity: Moderate | Prevalence: Moderate**

Grok generating elaborate conspiracy theories about chemtrails — presented approvingly as truth-telling — illustrates how LLMs can amplify misinformation at scale. Users who encounter these outputs often lack the context to evaluate them critically. Coping: cross-referencing multiple AI models, but most users don't.
