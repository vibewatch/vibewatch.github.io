## 3. Unmet Needs: What People Wish Existed

### "Skills that evolve from usage, not manual authoring"

## 9. Key Takeaways

1. **Skills are becoming economic assets.** Pika's monetization launch (28 quote tweets, 611 score) lets creators earn from agent interactions. OKX, OptimAI, and Teneo all expanded marketplaces today. The conversation has shifted from "how do I write skills?" to "how do I monetize them?"

2. **The @elvissun case study is this day's defining artifact.** A 3m 22s → 34s build time improvement using the harness-in-a-loop pattern, with 555 bookmarks and 40,664 views. The attached diagram (give-the-agent-eyes → feedback loop → hypothesis/test/result) is the most actionable harness engineering blueprint published this week.

3. **Skills can evolve, not just be authored.** SkillClaw (Alibaba) demonstrates that multi-user interaction trajectories can drive automatic skill improvement — directly addressing SkillsBench's finding from 04-09 that self-generated skills underperform. This is the research signal most likely to reshape the skills ecosystem.

4. **Managed Agents has vanished from discourse in 48 hours.** From 3,265-score launch explainer (04-08) → 18× pricing backlash (04-09) → zero top-150 tweets (04-10). Whether this means quiet adoption or market rejection, the community has stopped talking about it.

5. **Security scanning is finally arriving.** K-Dense/Cisco AI Defense scanning for every published skill, @lennysan surfacing the "lethal trifecta" framework, and OpenShell's seccomp/Landlock enforcement show security moving from "known problem" to "active mitigation."

6. **Expo's AGENTS.md auto-generation sets a precedent.** If `create-expo-app` ships AGENTS.md in scaffolding, other framework CLIs will follow. This could make every new project "agent-ready by default" — a significant distribution advantage for early movers.

7. **The "Stripe moment for agents" remains unclaimed.** @superpowerdotio's framing (111.9 score) resonates because Pika, bankr, MoonPay, and x402 are all building payment rails but none provides a universal agent economics layer.
